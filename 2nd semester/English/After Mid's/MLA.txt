**MLA (Modern Language Association) format** is used mainly in the **humanities**. It provides a consistent structure for writing, especially for:

* Document formatting
* In-text (parenthetical) citations
* A final Works Cited page (listing all sources)

---

## ðŸ”¹ **General MLA Guidelines**

* Use **12 pt. Times New Roman** font
* **Double-space** the entire document
* **1-inch margins** on all sides
* Indent paragraphs by **0.5 inches**
* **Page numbers** in top-right header with your **last name**
* Title is **centered** â€” no bold, underlining, or italics
* Use **italics** (not underlining) for longer works like books or films

---

## ðŸ”¹ **In-Text Citation: Core Concepts**

* MLA uses **author-page style**:

  > (Authorâ€™s Last Name Page Number)
  > e.g., (Smith 45)

* If no author:

  > Use a **shortened title** in quotation marks: ("Article Title" 6)

* In-text citation should match the first word(s) in the corresponding Works Cited entry.

---

## ðŸ”¹ **Works Cited Page: Core Concepts**

* Title the page **"Works Cited"**
* Entries are **alphabetized by author's last name**
* Use **hanging indentation** (first line flush left, next lines indented)

## ðŸ”¹ **Special Citation Cases**

* **No Author**: Start citation with the title
* **Multiple Authors**: List up to 2 authors; for 3 or more use **et al.**
* **Bible & Multivolume Works**: Cite book, chapter, and verse (e.g., Ezek. 1.5â€“10)
* **Indirect Sources**: Use **qtd. in** for quoting someone cited within another source
