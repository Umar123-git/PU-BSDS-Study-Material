## üî∑ WHAT IS APA STYLE?

APA (American Psychological Association) style is commonly used in **social sciences** and focuses on clarity, structure, and source attribution. It includes:

* In-text citations (brief references in the body)
* Reference list (detailed citations at the end)
* Specific paper formatting guidelines

---

## üî∑ GENERAL FORMATTING RULES (APA 7th Edition)

* Font: Times New Roman 12 pt (or other readable fonts)
* Spacing: Double-spaced
* Margins: 1 inch on all sides
* Page numbers: Top-right corner
* Title page, abstract, main body, and references
* Use **title case** and **bold** for headings

---

## üî∑ IN-TEXT CITATION (APA)

APA uses two styles:

1. **Narrative**: Author‚Äôs name appears in sentence
   üëâ *Smith (2020) found that...*

2. **Parenthetical**: Author and year in parentheses
   üëâ *(Smith, 2020)*

If quoting directly, include page number:
üëâ *(Smith, 2020, p. 23)*
üëâ *Smith (2020) stated, ‚Äú‚Ä¶‚Äù (p. 23).*

---

## üî∑ REFERENCE LIST RULES

* Titled **"References"** (bold, centered)
* Alphabetical by author's last name
* Use **hanging indent**
* Format: Author. (Year). *Title*. Source.
  Only the first word and proper nouns are capitalized in titles (sentence case).

## üî∑ FORMATS & EXAMPLES

---

### ‚úÖ **a. Book**

**In-text citation:**
*(King, 2019)*
or
*King (2019)*

**Reference format:**
Author, A. A. (Year). *Title of book: Subtitle if any*. Publisher.

**Example:**
King, S. (2019). *The institute: A novel*. Scribner.

---

### ‚úÖ **b. Journal Article (Print or Online)**

**In-text citation:**
*(Lee & Zhang, 2020)*
or
*Lee and Zhang (2020)*

**Reference format:**
Author, A. A., & Author, B. B. (Year). Title of article. *Title of Journal, volume number*(issue number), pages. [https://doi.org/xxxxx](https://doi.org/xxxxx)

**Example:**
Lee, J., & Zhang, Y. (2020). Mental health in college students. *Journal of Student Psychology*, 22(3), 150‚Äì162. [https://doi.org/10.1037/spq0000398]

---

### ‚úÖ **c. Article from an Online Periodical (e.g., News Website, Magazine)**

**In-text citation:**
*(Waters, 2022)*
or
*Waters (2022)*

**Reference format:**
Author, A. A. (Year, Month Day). Title of article. *Title of Website*. URL

**Example:**
Waters, L. (2022, July 10). How remote work is reshaping offices. *Forbes*. [https://www.forbes.com/remote-work](https://www.forbes.com/remote-work)

---

### ‚úÖ **d. YouTube Video**

**In-text citation:**
*(CrashCourse, 2019)*
or
*CrashCourse (2019)*

**Reference format:**
Name of account or group. (Year, Month Day). *Title of video* [Video]. YouTube. URL

**Example:**
CrashCourse. (2019, August 13). *Statistics: Crash course sociology #7* [Video]. YouTube. [https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Vfo5le26IhY](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Vfo5le26IhY)

## üî∑ CONCEPTS TO REMEMBER

| Feature                  | APA Rule                                                       |
| ------------------------ | -------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Authors**              | Last name + initials (Smith, J. A.)                            |
| **Year only**            | Use (2020), not full date (except for news articles or videos) |
| **Title Capitalization** | Sentence case for books/articles; Title Case for journals      |
| **Italicize**            | Book titles, journal names, video titles                       |
| **DOI or URL**           | Include for online sources if available                        |
| **No author?**           | Start with title; move it to author position                   |
| **3+ authors?**          | Use first name + *et al.* in in-text citation                  |
